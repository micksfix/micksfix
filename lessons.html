How I think about the swing

Most golfers have been handed a pile of swing tips that contradict each other. My approach is different: the swing is a sequence, and that sequence has to fit your body, not someone else’s. Years of playing and competing — and a long-running fascination with biomechanics and how the body generates and transfers energy — shape how I teach. We work on a swing you can actually repeat and that doesn’t break down under pressure or wear your body out.

What a lesson looks like

We start by watching how you really move, rather than forcing you into a one-size-fits-all template. From there we build the fundamentals that hold up: a sound setup, a sequence that delivers power efficiently, and a motion that protects your body instead of fighting it. The goal isn’t a textbook-perfect pose — it’s a swing that works for you and sends the ball where you want it.

Who it’s for

Total beginners who want to start the right way. Juniors building a foundation that will grow with them. Adults knocking off the rust or finally taking the game seriously. Experienced players chasing the details. All ages, all levels — all welcome.

Two languages

I teach in both English and Japanese. If you’re more comfortable learning in Japanese, that’s no problem at all — the lesson is yours.
