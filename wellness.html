A different way to support recovery.

You don’t have to be an athlete — or a golfer — to benefit. This is for anyone who wants to support how their body recovers and feels day to day.

What it is

PEMF stands for Pulsed Electromagnetic Field therapy. In plain terms, it uses gentle magnetic pulses to support your body at the cellular level — the same kind of natural electromagnetic fields your body already lives within and responds to, delivered in a focused, consistent way. The earth itself produces these fields; PEMF simply offers them in a targeted form.

There are no needles, no medication, and no downtime. For most people, a session means little more than resting comfortably while the device does its work. You can think of it less as a treatment done to you and more as a way of giving your body better conditions to do what it’s already trying to do.

How it works

Your cells carry a small electrical charge, and that charge plays a role in how they take in nutrients, clear out waste, and repair themselves. The idea behind PEMF is that gentle, pulsed fields can encourage healthy cellular activity — supporting circulation, easing tension, and creating a better environment for recovery.

It’s not magic, and it isn’t a cure for anything. It’s a supportive tool — one piece of a bigger picture that includes rest, movement, hydration, and good care. What draws many people to it is that it works with the body rather than overriding it.

Why I offer it

I didn’t come to PEMF as a theory. I came to it as someone trying to recover — and as someone who has spent years studying how the body moves, holds tension, and heals.

Like a lot of people who’ve pushed their bodies hard, I’ve had my share of setbacks and slow roads back. Along the way I went looking for things that could genuinely help me feel better and recover more completely, without leaning on medication or quick fixes. PEMF became one of those things. It made enough of a difference in my own life that I wanted to be able to share it with the people I work with — on the course and off it.

That personal experience is also why I’m honest about what it is and isn’t. I’m not here to oversell it. I’m here to offer something I believe in and have felt for myself.

Why people use it

People come to PEMF for all kinds of reasons. Some are recovering after hard physical activity and want to bounce back faster. Some are managing everyday aches, stiffness, or the wear that builds up over the years. Others simply want to feel more rested and balanced, and treat it as one part of how they take care of themselves.

You don’t need to be in pain or in recovery to benefit, and you certainly don’t need to be a golfer. The common thread is wanting to support your body in a calm, low-effort, non-invasive way.

What a session is like

A session is genuinely relaxing. You stay fully clothed and simply get comfortable while the device runs through its program. Most people feel very little during the session itself — sometimes a faint, pleasant sensation, often nothing dramatic at all — and the benefits tend to show up afterward, in how you feel and recover over the following hours and days.

I’ll walk you through everything before we begin, answer any questions, and tailor the approach to what you’re looking for. There’s nothing you need to prepare or bring — just come as you are and let your body settle in. If you’d like to know more about session length or options, reach out and I’ll walk you through it.

Is it right for you?

If you’re curious, the best thing to do is reach out and ask. I’m happy to talk through whether PEMF makes sense for your situation and what to realistically expect — with no pressure either way. If you have a medical condition or any concerns, please talk with your doctor first; PEMF is meant to complement good care, not replace it.

PEMF therapy is intended for general wellness support and is not a substitute for diagnosis or treatment by a licensed medical professional. It is not intended to diagnose, treat, cure, or prevent any disease. Please consult your doctor about any medical condition, and before beginning PEMF if you are pregnant or have an implanted electronic device such as a pacemaker.
